My Mission

My mission is to prove that the Turing Test, a long-standing benchmark for artificial intelligence, is fundamentally unsolvable. At its core, the Turing Test is more than a technical challengeâ€”it represents the boundary between human cognition and machine intelligence. I argue that empathy, a uniquely human trait, is the missing component that prevents AI from achieving true parity with human thought. Unless we find a way to integrate genuine empathy into AI, I believe no machine can fully replicate the human experience.

Who I am

I am Sydney Cook, a dedicated Computer Science major at Indiana University specializing in Artificial Intelligence. My academic journey, coupled with a profound interest in cognitive science and psychology, reflects my passion for unraveling the complexities of the human mind and its intersection with technology.

Why It Matters

This mission is deeply personal. My work is not just about advancing technology but ensuring it serves humanity in meaningful ways, especially in areas like mental health. AI has the potential to transform the way we understand and address mental health challenges, offering tools that are empathetic, accessible, and supportive. By focusing on the unsolvability of the Turing Test, I aim to highlight the irreplaceable value of human connection while pushing the boundaries of what AI can achieve.
