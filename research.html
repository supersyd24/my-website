PDF Title 1

This document presents my mathematical theorem, which explores the inherent limitations of artificial intelligence in replicating human consciousness. It utilizes Bayesian inference, fuzzy logic, and neural activation frameworks to model processes such as threat assessment, decision-making, and emotional regulation. By simulating how humans process trauma, the theorem underscores the critical roles of empathy, rapid neural plasticity, and biological mechanisms like extrasynaptic GABA receptor activation. Drawing connections to the dissociative subtype of PTSD and contrasting these processes with individuals who lack empathy, the theorem offers compelling evidence for the unsolvability of the Turing Test and illuminates the unique biological foundations of genuine human cognition.
